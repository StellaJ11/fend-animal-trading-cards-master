Puppies

Human newborns and puppies require close to the same amount of sleep. The average human newborn sleeps 16 hours in a 24-hour time period, and puppies need about 14 hours of sleep a day.

  • Feeding: When a puppy is small, he/she needs to eat five small portions of food a day.
  • Sensory: Puppies can't smell until three weeks post birth.
  • Lifestyle: When a puppy is first born, 90 percent of their life is spent sleeping while the other 10 percent is spent eating.
  • Sniffing: At 3 weeks old, puppies not only develop a keen sense of smell, but they're also pretty amazing sniffers.

When puppies are born, they don't open their eyes or begin to hear until they're 9 to 12 days old. Since a lot of important development and growth happens for puppies during the first 3 weeks, they spend 90% of their time sleeping and need to eat five small portions of food a day. After 3 weeks old, they develop their senses of smell, hear and see and start exploring the world.
